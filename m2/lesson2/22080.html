Lesson Summary

Planning for your recruiting event is, in the long run, what will make it successful.  Now you know how to research appropriate events, what needs to be done before you leave, and how to prepare yourself for recruiting.  You also have a list of what materials you should consider taking with you to the event.

Next, you’ll learn about what to do when you arrive at your recruiting event.

Researching a Specific Effort/Event

  • Clarify your needs
  • Identify possible events
    • Identify opportunities based on the populations you want to reach, given your needs
  • Coordinate with event hosts to evaluate the audience
    • Determine audience characteristics, drivers, motivators, expectations, and challenges
  • Analyze the event based on the cost and expected benefits to determine if NRCS should participate

 

Getting Approval and Selecting a Team

  • Always get approval from your manager before proceeding with recruitment efforts
  • Whenever possible, include two types of people on your team
    • Human Resources Representative(s)
    • Field Representative(s)

 

Gathering or Ordering Recruiting Materials

Gather the recruiting materials you will need. Consider:

  • Brochures and flyers about NRCS and USAJobs.gov
  • Exhibits, props, or demonstration materials
  • Give-aways
  • Business cards
  • Nametags
  • Sign-in sheets and interview schedules
  • Pay chart
  • Office supplies, such as pens, pencils, paperclips, and staplers
  • Hand sanitizer

Attire

Your attire should reflect the culture of the agency but also be appropriate for the event. Be sure to determine proper attire with the event organizer and communicate the choice to every member of the recruitment team.
