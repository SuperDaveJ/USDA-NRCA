Lesson Summary

Congratulations!  Now you can successfully attend a recruiting event or effort.  You know how to effectively promote NRCS, building credibility through your representation.  You know what to do when talking with potential applicants and how to present the agency in a realistic yet positive light.  You also learned tips for how to properly conduct an on-site interview.  And finally, you learned the appropriate actions to take following a recruiting event or effort.

The next module, Module 3, will enable you to speak intelligently to candidates about how NRCS hires its employees—the legal authorities through which all Federal employees are hired.

Representing Yourself and the Agency

Building Credibility
Establishing the credibility of the NRCS is your first goal at a recruiting event.  The NRCS needs to be seen as an opportunity to contribute to society and the environment in a very direct way.  Credibility is created by:

  • Making positive initial impressions
  • Talking with the candidates
  • Ask discovery questions
  • Focus on the mission of NRCS specifically
  • Present information clearly and appropriately
  • Discuss positions and job titles knowledgably
  • Knowing how to say, “I Don’t Know”

Managing Expectations
NRCS candidates need to have a realistic understanding of the nature of our work and how they would contribute to the NRCS, before they apply.  You can manage these expectations during recruiting by:

  • Being realistic and positive about the work we do
  • Describing benefits of working at NRCS, relative to the candidate
  • Informing the candidate about applying through USAJobs.gov
  • Avoiding negative discussions about NRCS or other agencies

Highlighting the benefits of the agency and, most importantly, how a potential applicant can positively contribute to the NRCS are the best ways to promote the agency. 

Informing Participants About Applying

If you are asked questions about the application process that you are not prepared to answer, you should refer applicants to the NRCS Human Resources department in your state.  Be sure to familiarize yourself with the application process for available positions as much as possible.  Make sure you know:

  • What the candidate should do to apply
  • What the position requirements are
  • What documents need to be included with the application
  • Contact information for who the candidate should speak to about questions
  • Where the candidate should send his or her application and required documents

Detailed information on applying at USAJobs.gov can be found at: http://www.usajobs.gov/infocenter

Interviewing Techniques and Procedures

Interviewing When Recruiting
If you anticipate interviewing during a recruiting event, you should make sure that an HR representative is on your team.  This will help ensure you have a resource on hand to clarify any hiring-specific questions.
If you conduct interviews at a recruiting event it’s important to prepare specifically for that function.  And, since it’s specifically regulated, know the do’s and don’ts when conducting interviews.

Interviewing Do’s

  • Ensure consistency by asking the same questions to all candidates
  • Make sure to use the same forms for each applicant
  • Ask job-related questions aimed at eliciting an applicant’s qualifications

Interview Don’ts

  • Ask illegal questions
  • Emphasize negative aspects of a position
  • Discourage the applicant from applying
  • Speculate on an applicant’s chances of being hired

Conducting the Interview
Interviews are conducted in four phases.  You need to be familiar with and practice each of the phases, which are:

  • Opening
  • Asking the interview questions
  • Providing information
  • Closing

After a Recruiting Event

You should assess the success and appropriateness of the event by evaluating: 

  • Were you able to reach and recruit the types of candidates you intended?
  • Was the event cost-effective?
  • Would you participate in this event or effort in the future?

Then, follow up with the official or department from whom you received the authorization to attend.

Ensure any resumes and interview information you collected are properly filed with Human Resources, and follow up with candidates as appropriate.
