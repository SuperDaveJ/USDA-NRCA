Student Candidate

I’ll graduate this year with a Master’s degree in Natural Resources Management. I see lots of demand for talent in the field of environmental conservation and that’s where I want to pursue a career. Why would I want to work for NRCS over another agency like EPA or Interior, or even private industry?

NRCS Recruiter

Can you tell me a little bit about why you worked toward your degree in Natural Resources Management in the first place? What interested you about the field?

Student Candidate

I’ve always been concerned about what we’re doing to the environment. Helping manage our natural resources is something I can do that will make a difference for our kids.

NRCS Recruiter

It sounds like you want to do something to make the world a better place. In that case, there’s nowhere better to have a satisfying career than in the Federal government.

Specifically at NRCS we offer challenging work that really pays off in impact on conservation practices that affect the environment. You’d have opportunities to develop your skills in an environment with a great deal of stability. We’re well known for having dedicated and talented people—people like you, who are committed to the conservation and preservation of our country’s natural resources.

We have three regions which include all 50 states, as well as territories in the Caribbean and Pacific. That means you could do real hands-on work in a variety of places. You would help farmers and ranchers, or communities in general, conserve soil, protect wildlife, restore water quality, or implement other measures to conserve the Nation’s natural resources for future generations.

You’d use your interpersonal skills to interact daily with people and encourage them to participate in conservation. At the end of the day you would be able to actually see the benefit of your work in the community where you serve.

Student Candidate

With private industry I could still work in the field and visit a lot of places, and wouldn’t I get a lot more perks?

NRCS Recruiter

The Government offers very competitive perks. We have flexible work schedules and programs to help employees balance the demands of work with personal and family responsibilities. The Government offers training, great benefits, and hiring incentives that compete with any company.

Private industry may offer you a bit more money or some flashy incentives to start, but for the long term, you can’t beat government work. That’s why most of our people stay with us for so long.
