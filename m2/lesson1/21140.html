Lesson Summary

You’re now familiar with the first steps to great recruiting!  You know how to focus your recruiting efforts to efficiently and effectively recruit well-qualified, representative candidates. 

You’ve broadened your definition of diversity and learned to consider this concept in different ways when thinking about your recruiting efforts.  You also learned about the different kinds of recruiting efforts and events, and you learned how to judge which ones best fit the needs of your specific recruiting needs.

General Requirements for Working at NRCS

The following are minimum requirements for working at NRCS:

  • Proof of U.S. Citizenship
  • Possession of a Valid Driver’s License
  • Attainment of Appropriate Educational Level
  • Clearance of a Background Check

Focus Your Efforts

Targeted recruiting focuses on reaching specific populations of well-qualified, diverse applicants who fit with the agency and positions we have to offer.  Potential candidates need to have acquired the technical expertise needed to perform their jobs with as little-needed supplemental training as possible.

To focus your efforts you need to:

  • Identify appropriate sources for candidates
  • Partner with schools, trade associations, or other organizations that draw candidates with particular skill sets in conservation and agriculture
  • Know what the qualifications are for each position within NRCS

Types of Recruiting Efforts

There are several types of events or efforts that can aid in your recruiting initiatives.  It will be up to you to decide which is best for your situation and the types of candidates you’re looking for.

  • School and Campus Relationships
  • Community Event Participation or Sponsorship
  • Professional Organizations and Societies
  • City Job Centers and Employment Offices
  • Career Fairs
  • Vacancy Announcements
  • Unplanned Opportunities

Create a Representative Workforce

NRCS aims to create a workforce that can draw on a multitude of backgrounds and experiences in order to develop solutions as diverse as the ecosystems we aim to conserve.  As we continue to focus on creating a highly-qualified, diverse workforce make sure you check the annual representation statistics so that you’re focusing efforts appropriately.

This necessitates creating a pool of eligible, highly skilled candidates to pull from when needed.  To do this, recruiters and HR representatives need to know where these candidates are; and, the candidates must know about NRCS, its vision, and the type of work environment it offers as a place for a career in conservation.
