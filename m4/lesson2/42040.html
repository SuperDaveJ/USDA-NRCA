Health, Dental, and Vision Insurance

Health, dental, and vision insurance are available to Federal government employees through the Federal Employees Health Benefit (FEHB) plan.  Upon appointment, new employees must elect or waive participation in the FEHB.  Then, an open season for new enrollment or enrollment changes in the FEHB is available once per year.

Through this program, costs of health insurance premiums are shared between the employee and the Federal government.  Dental and vision insurance are also offered through the FEHB; however, the employee pays the total premium for these services.

Working for the Federal government can also include the option to establish a Flexible Spending Account (FSA), which allows pre-tax dollars to go into an account to reimburse the employee for medical expenses not covered under FEHB.  Employees must be enrolled in FEHB to receive this benefit.
