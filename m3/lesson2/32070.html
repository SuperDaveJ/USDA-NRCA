FCIP Guidelines

The FCIP is designed to provide work experience for candidates who have completed a Bachelor’s or Graduate degree.  Employees working under the Federal Career Internship Program are appointed to 2-year internships.  FCIP positions are unique in that they provide:

  • Rotational assignments, where interns are offered different experiences throughout their two-year internship, affording them experience in careers across the agency.
  • Mentoring, where a current NCRS employee is assigned to guide their experience and learning while the new employee is an FCIP.  Interns must also create an Individual Development Plan (IDP) to outline their short- and long-term goals for the program.  The plan includes what professional development they would request or require in order to achieve those goals.
  • Formal training, in tandem with real-world work experience.

FCIP positions can be hired directly, depending on which GS level he or she would be hired to, and candidates must submit a Career Intern Application along with a resume and transcript.  FCIPs are appointed at the GS-5, 7, or 9 level based on their level of education.  NRCS has the option to non-competitively convert them to a career-conditional appointment at the end of the 2-year period based on the employee’s performance and conduct.  FCIPs are eligible for all Federal benefits.
