1890 Scholars Employee

I'm Drenda Williams and I am the Assistant State Conservationist for Operations in Missouri.  I was hired through the 1890 Scholarship program and had the opportunity of being accepted as one of the first group of student in 1993 when the program was established.  I learned about the program after my counselor was given the information by an Agriculture professor from my hometown who worked at an 1890 school.  Because I came from a rural school of approximately 250 high school students, networking and word of mouth advertisements about the program are very crucial.  After I was selected as a scholar and wanted to major in computer information systems, the NRCS served as my sponsoring agency.

One of the benefits of the program is that it provides an exceptional professional experience for the scholars.  Not only did it provide financial assistance for 4 years of college, but it also allowed me to work as an intern during the summer months as well as during the school year.  I worked in approximately 6 field offices as a Soil Conservationist trainee prior to graduating, and performed services in the areas of conservation planning, Geographic Information Systems, surveying, database management, and practice compliance.  And while I was an intern, the hours I worked counted toward years of service after I was converted to a permanent employee.

I am very grateful for the 1890 program and the opportunities it has afforded me as an NRCS employee.  I have been able to progress professionally to a manager level and look to be a State Conservationist in the future.  As a former 1890 scholar, I have also charged myself to be a marketing tool and example of the benefits of the program and look forward to its continuation to develop numerous conservation professionals for years to come.
