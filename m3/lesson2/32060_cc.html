FCIP Employee

Hi, my name is Phuong Ly from California. After I graduated from California State Polytechnic University of Pomona, I was fortunate to be hired into the NRCS Career Intern Program.  I'm currently at the end of my two-year FCIP as a soil conservationist.

FCIP is a wonderful program because it provides a variety of training to help me transition to a permanent position with the agency.  As part of the FCIP, the Employee Development Plan (or EDP) describes the skills and abilities necessary for doing the job. The EDP includes certain trainings that I'm required to take, such as New Employee Orientation which sets the basic NRCS background and foundation. Also, the EDP has been modified to include trainings that are to my interests or that help me do a better job.

Another great thing about the FCIP program is that every six months there is an evaluation form to fill out from Human Resources (HR).  The evaluation form is a great tool to identify how I am doing and for providing feedback for future FCIPs.  Besides the biannual report, an assigned mentor was given to me to provide additional assistance to guide me in the right direction for the agency.
