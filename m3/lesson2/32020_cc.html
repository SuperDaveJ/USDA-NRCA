STEP Employee

Hi! My name is Andy McCartney. I’m a student at the University of Michigan--Go Blue!--and I was lucky enough to be hired as a STEP intern at the NRCS. This program has offered me several opportunities that will be invaluable as I go forward with my academic and professional career. For starters, it’s a chance to experience a real-world office environment without being confined to a copy room.  I have the opportunity to work on numerous projects reflecting both organizational policies, and operations. Being directly involved gives me a better understanding of the goals here at the NRCS, and allows me to see where my contribution plays a part in the success of its mission. Simply the ability to use some of my skills outside of an academic arena, the chance to see how they translate into the professional world, has been a boon to my self-confidence and given me more options for my future planning.

The most rewarding part of STEP—even more so than the paycheck—is the chance to operate in a professional work environment with colleagues who are not only willing to pass along valuable knowledge about the agency, but about their own professional experiences. These lessons are a great source of information for a student like me; they reflect a vast amount of institutional experience that I can take with me going forward, and I’m grateful for the opportunity.
