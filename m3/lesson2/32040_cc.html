STEP Employee

I’m Edwin Martinez.  I’m currently working fulltime as a District Conservationist in Clinton County, Michigan and working part-time on my doctoral degree at Michigan State University. I was initially hired through the Student Career Experience Program in 2005 while I was finishing my graduate degree at the University of Puerto Rico. I was introduced to the NRCS during a national recruiting effort in the fall of 2005.  The recruiting experience opened my eyes to the work this great agency does and allowed me to learn about all the opportunities available nationwide.

Conservation of natural resources was my passion and finding an opportunity with the NRCS was one of my priority student goals. In 2005 I achieved this milestone when I received the opportunity to work in North Platte, Nebraska during the summer as a Soil Conservationist Student Trainee. I had the opportunity to finish the 640 hours required by the SCEP program in the Utuado, PR field office.

Once I completed my degree I was hired as a permanent employee and started my career as a Soil Conservationist in Tulelake, California where I received excellent training opportunities and completed several details working under the Emergency Watershed Protection Program in multiple locations within California. I worked in the San Bernardino and San Diego counties, assisting with conservation planning before and after fire emergencies.

The SCEP program was a great experience which definitely helped me to achieve my career and professional goals. The program is an excellent learning experience and a unique opportunity to expose students to what we do as an agency. Experiences like being in the field with NRCS professionals, utilization of technology, participation in training and conferences, and the flexibility of this agency were determinant in the positive impact that SCEP had on my career. I started my career through this program and I have continued promoting this opportunity everywhere I have worked and lived (Puerto Rico, Nebraska, California, Oregon, and Michigan) because it is a unique recruitment and retention method.
