Lesson Summary

As you can see, there are multitudes of ways NRCS can employ fresh, bright talent, and support individuals as they begin and develop their careers in areas of conservation.

You learned about the differences between the STEP, SCEP, and FCIP student programs.  You also learned about several scholarship opportunities available from NRCS to college students.  These programs are important to understand, as together they provide much of the new talent that NRCS hires.

STEP Guidelines

STEP employees are students working on a temporary basis for NRCS.  These employees must be:

  • Enrolled at least half-time in an accredited high school,
  • Technical or vocational school, or
  • A 2- or 4-year college or university.

Students of any discipline can be hired directly as STEPs, allowing more flexibility for both students and managers.  STEPs cannot be converted into full-time employees; however, they can be converted into SCEPs.

Student Career Experience Program (SCEP) Guidelines

SCEP positions provide relevant work experience and exposure to public service while enhancing the student’s educational goals and shaping his or her career choices.  They are assigned to positions with responsibilities that tie to their educational discipline.

SCEPs:

  • Can be hired directly
  • Must be enrolled in a qualifying educational institution
  • Must provide a resume and transcripts
  • Are not considered temporary and, therefore, receive all Federal benefits 

SCEPs can be converted into full-time employees within 120 days of completing their academic requirements.  They must have completed 640 hours of work as a SCEP.

FCIP Guidelines

The FCIP is designed to provide work experience for candidates who have completed a Bachelor’s or Graduate degree.  FCIP positions can be hired directly, depending on the GS level to be hired to, and candidates must submit a Career Intern Application along with a resume and transcript.

FCIP positions are unique in that they provide:

  • Rotational assignments, where interns are offered different experiences throughout their two-year internship, affording them experience in careers across the agency
  • Mentoring, where a current NCRS employee is assigned to guide their experience and learning while the new employee is an FCIP 
  • Formal training, in tandem with real-world work experience

Scholarship Programs

NRCS supports the following three scholarship programs for students:

  • 1890 Scholars Program
  • USDA Public Service Leaders Scholarship Program
  • USDA Tribal College Scholarship Program
