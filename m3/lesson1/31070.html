Lesson Summary

You now have a general familiarity with the different hiring authorities available to NRCS.  Now let’s get into more detail about some of the programs, starting with the student and internship programs in Lesson 2.

Recruitment Intensive Hiring Authorities

NRCS actively recruits for student and internship program candidates on a regular basis.  Recruiting activity levels vary for non-competitive hires and potential applicants qualifying under Veterans, Schedule A, and Delegated Examining Unit (DEU) authorities.

DEU
Delegated Examining, or competitive recruiting, is required in order to consider candidates who are not former Federal employees or who are not eligible for appointment under one of the non-competitive authorities.

Student and Internship Programs
There are three primary student or internship programs that NRCS utilizes:

  • Student Temporary Employment Program (STEP)
  • Student Career Employment Program (SCEP)
  • Federal Career Intern Program (FCIP) 

Each program has varied eligibility requirements and conditions for conversion to full-time employment status. 

Alternative Hiring Programs

There are other programs which NRCS uses to acquire staff, but for which it does not actively recruit.  However, individuals may qualify for employment under these programs:

  • Agriculture Conservation Experienced Services (ACES) Program
  • When Actually Employed (WAE)
  • Student Conservation Association (SCA)

Merit Promotion and Reinstatement

Reinstatement applies to candidates who have held a career or career-conditional appointment in the past.
