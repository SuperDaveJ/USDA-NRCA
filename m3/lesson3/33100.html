What Would the Expert Say?

Here’s your chance to practice describing some of these hiring authorities.  Type in your answer to this potential applicant’s question, “I’ve heard it’s really hard to get a job with the government if you don’t get your foot in the door as an intern or don’t qualify for one of the special considerations like being a veteran.  How can I apply for a position with NRCS?”
Please scan USA jobs for positions that are open to “U.S. Citizens”.  Read the qualification requirements and respond directly to the questions asked, providing documentation in your resume that supports the responses.  Getting a foot in the door is exactly the right approach.  Apply for jobs that may be lower graded and in less than the most desirable locations.  It is all about the competition and who you might be competing against for that job.  Once you are on a career conditional appointment, you attain that “status” candidate designation and the entire Federal government is open to you.  Not everyone hired is a veteran or a student appointed. 
Type in your response, and then select DONE.
