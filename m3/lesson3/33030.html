Veterans' Recruitment Appointment (VRA)

VRA can also be used to fill temporary (not to exceed 1 year) or term (more than 1 year but not to exceed 4 years) positions. If a veteran is employed in a temporary or term position under VRA, he or she will not be converted to the competitive service after 2 years.

Audio Transcript

VRA is an excepted authority that allows agencies to appoint eligible veterans without competition. If a candidate:

  • Is in receipt of a campaign badge for service during a war or in a campaign or expedition; OR
  • Is a disabled veteran, OR
  • Is in receipt of an Armed Forces Service Medal for participation in a military operation, OR
  • Is a recently separated veteran (within the last 3 years) with at least 180 consecutive days of military service, AND
  • Separated under honorable conditions (this means an honorable or general discharge), that applicant is VRA eligible.

Veteran applicants can be appointed under this authority at any grade level up to and including a GS-11 or equivalent. This is an excepted service appointment, meaning that after successfully completing 2 years, the employee will be converted to the competitive service.
