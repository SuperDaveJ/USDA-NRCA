Lesson Summary

Now you’re familiar with the authorities by which NRCS hires its employees.  As you can see, there’s a wide variety of routes to employment with NRCS, and several ways to give qualifying applicants a competitive advantage when applying for Federal positions. 

Use your new-found knowledge when you’re recruiting to encourage and inform interested candidates.  Don’t forget to share the stories you have heard in this module, and draw from your own experience. 

Veteran Hiring Authorities

Three special hiring authorities exist for U.S. veterans.  These authorities are:

  • Veterans’ Recruitment Appointment (VRA) Authority
  • Veterans Employment Opportunities Act (VEOA) Authority
  • 30% or More Disabled Veteran authority

They are designed to benefit citizens who have previously served in the Armed Forces and give them special appointing consideration when applying for Federal positions. 

Veterans' Recruitment Appointment (VRA)

VRA is an excepted authority that allows agencies to appoint eligible veterans without competition.  If a candidate:

  • Is in receipt of a campaign badge for service during a war or in a campaign or expedition; OR
  • Is a disabled veteran, OR
  • Is in receipt of an Armed Forces Service Medal for participation in a military operation, OR
  • Is a recently separated veteran (within the last 3 years) with at least 180 consecutive days of military service, AND
  • Separated under honorable conditions (this means an honorable or general discharge), that applicant is VRA eligible.

Veterans Employment Opportunity Act (VEOA)

VEOA is a competitive service appointing authority that can only be used when filling permanent, competitive service positions. 
To be eligible for a VEOA appointment, the applicant’s latest discharge must be issued under honorable conditions, meaning an honorable or general discharge, AND he or she must be either:

  • A “preference eligible”, as defined in Title 5 U.S.C. 2108(3), which includes those family members entitled to derived preference, OR
  • A veteran who completed 3 or more years of active service.

30% or More Disabled Veteran

The 30% or More Disabled Veteran authority allows any veteran with a 30% or more service-connected disability to be non-competitively appointed.
Eligible applicants:

  • Are retired from active military service with a service-connected disability rating of 30% or more; OR
  • Have a rating by the Department of Veterans Affairs showing a compensable service-connected disability of 30% or more.

Military Spouses

An Executive Order in 2008 authorized the non-competitive appointment of certain military spouses to competitive service positions.  Qualifying spouses are those who:

  • Are relocating with their service-member spouse as a result of Permanent Change of Station (PCS) orders, OR
  • Are spouses of service members who incurred a 100% disability because of the service member's active duty service, OR
  • Are spouses of service members who were killed while on active duty.

Schedule A Hiring

The Schedule A hiring authority allows individuals with severe physical disabilities, psychiatric disabilities, or mental retardation to apply for non-competitive appointment.  These applicants must provide the following documentation of their eligibility under Schedule A:

  • Proof of disability from licensed medical professionals, state or private vocational rehabilitation specialists, or any Government agency that issues or provides disability benefit, and
  • Certification from the same individuals that provided the proof of disability stating that the applicant is likely to succeed in the performance of the duties of the position for which he or she is applying.

Delegated Examining Unit (DEU)

If an applicant does not qualify under any of the previous hiring authorities, he or she may still be considered for employment through the process of the DEU. The DEU is the result of an authority that allows the Federal government to recruit and hire employees from the general public.
