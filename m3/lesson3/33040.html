Veteran Hiring Authorities

VEOA of 1998, as amended, is a competitive service appointing authority that can only be used when filling permanent, competitive service positions. It cannot be used to fill excepted service positions. It allows veterans that are not currently Federal employees to apply to merit promotion announcements that are only open to current competitive service employees, referred to as “status” candidates, when the agency is recruiting outside its own workforce.  VEOA is not applicable if the vacancy announcement is limited to agency employees.

To be eligible for a VEOA appointment, the applicant’s latest discharge must be issued under honorable conditions, meaning an honorable or general discharge, AND he or she must be either:

  • A “preference eligible”, as defined in Title 5 U.S.C. 2108(3), which includes those family members entitled to derived preference, OR
  • A veteran who completed 3 or more years of active service.
