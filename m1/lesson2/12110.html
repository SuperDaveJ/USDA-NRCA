NRCS Mission and Principles

The agency’s mission, to provide products and services to people and organizations concerned with conservation of the nation’s natural resources, is supported by three guiding principles:

  • Service
  • Partnership
  • Technical Excellence

NRCS History

At the beginning, Hugh Hammond Bennett’s experience, experiments, and writings contributed to the creation of the Soil Erosion Service of the Interior Department.

We evolved into what the NRCS is today through the following steps:

  • 1933 - Soil Erosion Service began working with farmers in the Coon Creek watershed of southwestern Wisconsin to transform the square, eroding fields. 
  • 1935 - Passage of PL74-46 created the Soil Conservation Services (SCS) within the USDA.  SCS expanded to establish nearly 300 conservation districts and field offices, working with landowners.
  • 1994 - The SCS was renamed the Natural Resource Conservation Service (NRCS) to reflect the shift the service’s mission had taken to encompass soil, water, plants, and animals.

NRCS Today

Today, NRCS: 

  • Has a more holistic view of conservation.
  • Assists landowners of all kinds in conserving all kinds of natural resources. 
  • Contributes to tools and standards in agronomy, economics, forestry, and wildlife management. 
  • Focuses specific efforts depending on the nature of the specific conservation district.
