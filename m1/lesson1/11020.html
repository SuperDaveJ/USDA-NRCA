What is Recruiting?

So what exactly is recruiting?  To begin answering that question, let’s distinguish between recruiting and hiring.  Hiring involves selecting and formally employing an individual.  While in some cases recruiting may be the first step toward hiring, recruiting is more than just finding employees.  It’s also about promoting NRCS, providing information, and building relationships. 

Typical recruiting activities include:

  • Building relationships within the community with organizations such as high schools, colleges, and agricultural organizations.
  • Educating potential applicants about the agency and types of positions available.
  • Attracting a pool of highly-qualified, diverse candidates in order to meet future hiring needs and preparing applicants for the hiring process.
  • Building “brand-name” recognition for now and for the future.
  • Informal or unplanned recruiting moments of talking about what you do and what NRCS is all about.
