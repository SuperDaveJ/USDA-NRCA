Lesson Summary

Congratulations - you’re beginning your journey to becoming a superstar recruiter!

You understand that recruiting is more than just hiring—it’s identifying needs, locating possible skilled candidates, and conveying the mission and brand of the agency.

Now that you understand what recruiting is and why it’s necessary, let’s equip you with some of the tools you’ll need to recruit effectively.  One of the first things you need to know is general information about the agency and what it’s like to work in various positions at NRCS.

What is Recruiting?

Recruiting is:

  • The process that occurs before a person is selected and hired for employment.
  • The professional means of promoting NRCS, providing information to prospects, and building relationships with what may be future colleagues. 

Typical Recruiting Activities

  • Building “brand-name” recognition for now and for the future.
  • Building relationships within the community with organizations such as high schools, colleges, and agricultural organizations.
  • Educating potential applicants about the agency and different positions available.
  • Attracting a pool of highly-qualified, diverse candidates in order to meet future hiring needs and preparing applicants for the hiring process.
  • Informal or unplanned recruiting moments of talking about what you do and what NRCS is all about.

Why Recruit?

Bottom line – the NRCS must have access to the right people with the right skills at the right time.

To attract this workforce takes more than simple hiring.  Recruiters need to create and enhance the image of the NRCS as the “Best Place to Work.”  You must work at getting the word out about the agency, what we do, and what the future holds.

The NRCS Workforce

Within the next 5 years, nearly 80% of the employees in mission critical positions, as well as nearly 75% of the workforce at the executive level, will be eligible for retirement.

This necessitates creating a pool of eligible, highly skilled candidates to pull from when needed.  To do this, recruiters and HR representatives need to know where these candidates are; and, the candidates must know about NRCS, its vision, and the type of work environment it offers as a place for a career in conservation.
